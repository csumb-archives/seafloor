Supporting marine stewardship through science, technology and education

The Seafloor Mapping Lab (SFML), within the Division of Science and Environmental Policy at California State University Monterey Bay, specializes in high-resolution acoustic remote sensing for coastal habitats. Combining research and education with state-of-the-art geospatial technology, the SFML offers unique hands-on, field-to-finish experience to students while conducting professional habitat mapping surveys for resource management and basic research along the continental margins.

The successful execution of this mission has made SFML an effective collaborator with a variety of resource agencies and applied research consortiums. Data from these collaborations have been used to monitor coastal erosion, assess tsunami potential, identify essential fisheries habitat, design and manage marine protected areas, develop tools for the sustainable management of California's squid and rockfish fisheries, facilitate ecosystem-based management within the Elkhorn Slough National Estuarine Research Reserve and Morro Bay, and maintain shipping channels and facilitate beach replenishment at the mouth of San Francisco Bay.

As part of SFML efforts for data sharing, product development, and integration with other programs, SFML continues to evaluate the development and performance of its data support systems (servers, web-user access and data downloads through HTTP Data Library and Catalog, and recent integration within the Google Ocean model) for serving SFML acoustic remote sensing data products.
