CSMP -- California Seafloor Mapping Program web site

CSMP is a collaborative, multi-institutional campaign creating the first comprehensive, high-resolution basemap of California's state waters (shoreline out to 3 nmi)

CAPABILITIES

Hydrographic Survey Vessels
The R/V Harold Heath is a custom built, shallow draft, 35 ft. aluminum hydrographic survey catamaran. [vessel specs]


The R/V VenTresca is a custom built, shallow draft, 35 ft. aluminum hydrographic survey catamaran. [vessel specs]


 

The R/V MacGinitie is a 30 ft. trailerable survey vessel for use anywhere along the US West Coast. [vessel specs]

 


KelpFly
Modified JetSki system with sonar mount. [more info]


 

Mulitbeam Bathymetry
High-resolution bathymetric mapping. [more info]


 

Marine Laser Scanner
A vessel-mounted mobile marine LIDAR system for collecting high-resolution coastal features data (intertidal shoreline, offshore rocks and pinnacles). [more info]


Remotely Operated Vehicle
Georeferenced and quantitative video mapping of seafloor habitats and communities. [more info]



SeaBotix LBV 200L ROV
Standard MiniROV system with added vertical thrust and stability capabilities. [more info]



Side Scan Sonar
Digital mosaics and substrate classification from acoustic imagery. [more info]


 

Sub-bottom Profiling
Full spectrum sub-bottom chirp profiling for determination of seafloor sediment and rock layer thickness. [more info]


Sediment Grain Size Analyses
Automated particle size distribution analyses [particle counts, diameter, and areas with volume and weight representations] for habitat mapping and ground-truthing. [more info]


GIS Analysis and Image Processing
Quantitative habitat classification, species/habitat probability modeling, 3-D rendering and fly-throughs, GIS/video data fusion. [more info] [example]


GPS Base Station Data
Trimble reference station (TRS) GPS base station data from two separate receivers are posted and available for download via the SFML web site. [more info]

Access to SFML data via an HTTP download page. Data are organized by survey location/project and data type. [download here]
