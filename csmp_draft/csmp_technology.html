CSMP Technology

Multibeam Echo Sounder (MBES) surveysMultibeam bathymetry sonar technology uses multiple beams of sound simultaneously to cover a large fan-shaped area of the sea floor. Multibeam systems can have more than 100 transducers, arranged in precise geometrical patterns, sending out a swath of sound and recording the different return times. The return signals are received and converted to water depths (via acquisition software), and plotted as bathymetric soundings. The high-resolution (dense) data acquired by multibeam systems are complex, and can provide 100% coverage of the seafloor, requiring involved signal processing in order to interpret the data. Multibeam bathymetry provides the basic raw data being used by CSMP to define topographical features such as sediment interfaces, rock outcrops, and important seafloor habitat.

Airborne LIDAR Bathymetry (ALB)Airborne LIDAR Bathymetry (ALB) is used to survey areas too shallow for safe or efficient coverage by conventional vessel-based acoustic survey methods. The SHOALS-1000T Airborne LIDAR Sensor produces imagery that can be assessed similiar to sidescan sonar or multibeam backscatter....is operated from a Beechcraft King Air A90 aircraft (or equivalent) to acquire the dataset using the 1kHz bathymetric green laser. Prior to LIDAR acquisition, ground control stations are established and referenced. During LIDAR operations, dual frequency GPS receivers are set-up on the established ground control points and are operational for all data acquisition flights, allowing for post processed inertially aided kinematic positioning of all data.

Ground-Truthing DataGround truthing is conducted via video and still camera imagery and/or physical sediment samples to inform and validate the interpretation of the acoustic and LIDAR data products.

CSMP Mission

The creation of a high-resolution 1:24,000 scale geologic and habitat base map series covering all of California's 14,500 km2 state waters out to the 3 mile limit, and support of the state's Marine Life Protection Act Initiative (MLPA) goal to create a statewide network of Marine Protected Areas (MPAs).

Map generation products follow a 3-tiered approach-- from basic survey data to highly detailed habitat classification.


The need for baseline maps for monitoring & assessment was the most common requirement expressed among a very diverse list of mapping user needs.
